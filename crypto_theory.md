## 1. Information-Theoretic Blindness (The Dimension Problem)

In computationally secure systems, an attacker lacks the time or computing resources to calculate the solution (e.g., finding the prime factors of a 4096-bit integer). However, the solution is uniquely determined by the ciphertext; if Eve has infinite computing power, she can easily find it.

In our system, the solution **does not exist in the available information**. It is a problem of dimensional underdetermination, not computing power.

*   **She possesses a single equation with three unknown elements ($S, K_{pool}$, and the polynomial slope $a$).**

Even if Eve possesses an infinite quantum supercomputer capable of checking all possible values instantly, she learns nothing. Why? Because for **every single possible guess** she makes on the secret $S' \in \mathbb{Z}_{p}$, there exists an absolutely valid and unique corresponding value for $K_{pool}'$ and $a'$ that makes her guess mathematically correct. 

Because all these possibilities are perfectly uniform, Eve is faced with a flat probability distribution. She has zero statistical leverage; the information simply does not exist in the physical universe.

## 2. Shannon’s "Perfect Secrecy"

Claude Shannon proved in 1949 that a system is **information-theoretically secure** if the ciphertext $C$ reveals absolutely no information about the plaintext $M$. 

*   In a computationally secure system, checking all keys yields only one readable English plaintext (e.g., "Top Secret Document"), while all other keys yield garbage bytes. Eve can easily identify the correct key.
*   In our Shamir Secret Sharing layer over $\mathbb{Z}_{2^{31}-1}$, checking all possible polynomials yields **every single possible message** of that length. If Eve intercepts $k-1$ shares, her infinite computer can reconstruct a polynomial revealing "Secret Plans" under one key, "A Baking Recipe" under another, "Weather Reports" under a third, and "A Shopping List" under a fourth.
*   All these plaintexts are **100% mathematically consistent and equally probable**. 
*   **Conclusion:** Infinite computing power does not help Eve because she cannot distinguish the real message from the infinite sea of other plausible messages.

## 5. Defensive Vision against Compromised / Malicious Hardware (Kleptography)

If a backdoored hardware processor attempts to sneakily exfiltrate bits of our master keys or seed by subtly modulating the least significant bits of the public Wegman-Carter tags $T$ or coordinates $X$ (known as *Subliminal Kleptographic Leakage*), our software is designed to pass through a **Cryptographic Reverse Firewall (CRF)**. 
- A CRF is a simple, physically clean, and formally verified hardware gate-array placed between the endpoint and the router.
- Leveraging the homomorphic and linear properties of our finite field $\mathbb{Z}_{2^{31}-1}$, the CRF dynamically injects fresh, unmanipulated random scalar factors directly into the outgoing points $X$ and tags $T$ on-the-fly.
- This process preserves the underlying mathematical consistency required for Bob's transposition but **completely destroys and overwrites any subliminal leakage channels**, neutralizing hardware trojans instantly.

### Mitigation 2: Optical/Visual Shamir Secret Sharing (VSSS)

- The SSS-shares are printed steganographically onto physical, transparent film overlays (transparencies).

- The decryption is performed entirely in the analog, optical world—completely bypassing any computer microprocessor or digital RAM.

To prevent backdoored Hardware Random Number Generators (TRNGs) from generating "biased" or predictable nonces/polynomial coefficients designed to leak key bits over time:
